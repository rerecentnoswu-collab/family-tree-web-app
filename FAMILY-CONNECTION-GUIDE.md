# Family Tree Connection Guide

## Overview

This guide explains how new accounts can connect to existing family trees and how existing users can invite family members to join their family tree. The system is designed with security and privacy best practices while enabling seamless family connections.

## How Family Connections Work

### Security & Privacy First
- **Data Isolation**: Each user maintains their own copy of family data
- **Invitation-Based**: Connections require explicit invitations for security
- **User Control**: Users control who can access their family information
- **Privacy Protection**: Personal data remains private and secure

### Connection Methods
1. **Direct Invitations**: Family members send email invitations
2. **Automatic Detection**: Smart matching for potential family members
3. **Manual Acceptance**: Users choose which invitations to accept

---

## For New Users (Children/Family Members)

### Step 1: Sign Up
1. Create an account with your email address
2. Verify your email if required
3. Log in to your new account

### Step 2: Check for Family Connections
When you first log in with an empty family tree, the system automatically:

1. **Checks for Pending Invitations**
   - Looks for invitations sent to your email
   - Displays invitation cards with relationship details
   - Shows who invited you and the relationship type

2. **Searches for Potential Family Members**
   - Analyzes your email for name patterns
   - Looks for matching family members across accounts
   - Shows potential matches with confidence levels

### Step 3: Accept Family Invitations
If you have pending invitations:

1. **Review the Invitation**
   - See who invited you (parent, sibling, spouse, etc.)
   - View the relationship type and confidence
   - Check the inviter's information

2. **Accept the Invitation**
   - Click "Accept" on the invitation card
   - Wait for the family tree to be imported
   - Your family tree will populate automatically

3. **Enjoy Your Family Tree**
   - View your complete family relationships
   - See parents, siblings, and extended family
   - Add additional family members as needed

### What Happens When You Accept
- **Family Tree Import**: All family members are copied to your account
- **Relationship Preservation**: Parent-child and couple relationships maintained
- **Privacy Maintained**: You get your own copy, not direct access to others' data
- **Full Functionality**: Complete access to all family tree features

---

## For Existing Users (Parents/Family Members)

### Step 1: Send Family Invitations
1. **Go to the Family Tree page** (`/tree`)
2. **Click "Send Invitation"** in the invitation card
3. **Select the Family Member** to invite from your tree
4. **Choose Relationship Type** (parent, child, spouse, sibling)
5. **Enter Email Address** of the family member
6. **Click "Send Invitation"**

### Step 2: Invitation Management
After sending invitations:

1. **Track Status**: Invitations show as "pending" until accepted
2. **Send Reminders**: Resend invitations if needed
3. **Manage Access**: Control who has access to your family data

### Step 3: Family Growth
When family members accept:

1. **Automatic Connection**: They get your family tree data
2. **Relationship Updates**: Their tree connects to yours
3. **Shared History**: Everyone sees the complete family story

## Connection Types & Best Practices

### Parent-Child Connections
- **Use Case**: Parents inviting children to join
- **Best Practice**: Send from parent's account
- **Relationship**: Select "child" when inviting
- **Result**: Child sees parents and extended family

### Spouse Connections
- **Use Case**: Married couples connecting trees
- **Best Practice**: Both spouses send invitations to each other
- **Relationship**: Select "spouse" when inviting
- **Result**: Combined family tree with both sides

### Sibling Connections
- **Use Case**: Brothers and sisters connecting
- **Best Practice**: Any sibling can send the invitation
- **Relationship**: Select "sibling" when inviting
- **Result**: Shared view of parents and family

### Extended Family
- **Use Case**: Grandparents, aunts, uncles, cousins
- **Best Practice**: Use appropriate relationship type
- **Relationship**: Choose closest relationship type
- **Result**: Extended family network connections

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

#### "I don't see any invitations"
**Solution**: 
- Check the email address matches exactly
- Ask the inviter to resend the invitation
- Verify you're using the correct email account

#### "The invitation shows wrong relationship"
**Solution**:
- Contact the inviter to correct the relationship type
- Accept the invitation and update relationships manually
- Ask for a new invitation with correct type

#### "My family tree is incomplete"
**Solution**:
- Check if all family members have been invited
- Verify relationships are correctly set
- Add missing members manually after accepting

#### "I can't find the family member to invite"
**Solution**:
- Add the family member to your tree first
- Ensure they have a complete profile
- Use the correct spelling and information

### Error Messages

#### "Invitation not found"
- The invitation may have expired
- Check the invitation link or email
- Ask for a new invitation

#### "Access denied"
- You're not the intended recipient
- Use the correct email address
- Contact the inviter if needed

#### "Data sync error"
- Check your internet connection
- Try refreshing the page
- Contact support if issues persist
